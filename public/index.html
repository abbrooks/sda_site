About us

We go where the people go!
Sitting Duck Advertising was started in 2012 by its two founders with one simple question to answer…. Where do I put my drink and purse when I use a public bathroom at my favorite watering hole? This was a simple concept that started the ideation around not only how to solve this obvious issue, but also how to monetize the solution for both their customers and advertisers alike. With a deep background in marketing and serial enteurnerialship, Brian and Kim we able to turn a simple media frame combined with a shelf element into an ongoing revenue generating advertising medium that would benefit both their company and the venues that adopted their platform.

Through the last 4 years the founders began to realize that while their solution was a great way for locations to spruce up their venues with a very valuable answer to the question of “What do I look at while I’m doing my own business?”, the scalability and process was limited to just a local market with a limited “one stream” marketing source per location. They began looking for technology answers to make their proven business model a very expandable, and even more lucrative medium for their venues and advertising partners.

Since early 2015, our founders have shifted their focus to the Digital Sitting Duck platform to not only innovate and evolve our offering, but to also look for ways to even better improve upon the restroom experience for our customers’ customer. They have introduced new technology focused partners to provide a more interactive solution for the bathroom, while helping build a foundation for a more robust information driven advertising solution based on real data driven analytics. While our focus is a solution for a relatively untapped yet proven niche market, our goal is to stretch our educated marketing medium platform outside of the restroom to set a new standard for out of home marketing strategy. We believe we can provide accurate impression data combined with industry best practice demographic assessment to tell our advertisers who is truly seeing their advertisements and for how long. Where do we go from here? I guess you’ll just have to go to the bathroom to find out….
